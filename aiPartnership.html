The AI Partnership Puzzle: Assembling Without a Manual

Miska Braun November 27, 2025 6 min read AI

My today's two cents on AI.

I've spent the past year not learning to code better (that's still on the to-do list üòÑ and I still can't code), but in back-to-back AI conversations with Microsoft, GitHub, AWS, and a whole ecosystem of partners.

And honestly? It often feels like we're all trying to assemble IKEA furniture‚Ä¶ except nobody has the manual. Or they had it, lost it, or used it to level a table.

Everyone wants "One AI for me please." Very few are ready for the realities. Here goes:

Ownership chaos

AI sits in every department ‚Äî and in none of them.

  • IT: "We have the platform, so it's ours."
  • Developers: "Agents! We have them. They have us."
  • Security: "Put it in a concrete vault immediately."
  • Management: "AI, but safe, fast, magical‚Ä¶ and preferably free."

I've even seen teams arguing about who owns AI before discussing what they actually want to achieve with it.

GTM for product + services = spicy chaos

Product companies want neat, packaged AI solutions that "just onboard instantly." Services companies want to explore, customise, and fix the mess underneath.

AI sits in the middle whispering: "Good luck aligning those sales and GTM motions üòà"

This slows more deals than any skill gap or tech gap ever has.

AI moves faster than governance

Partners want to co-sell.

Vendors want to scale.

Customers want guarantees nobody can actually give.

Teams align on a webinar about "the need for AI," get excited‚Ä¶ and then realise they forgot pricing, delivery, support, or basically everything that matters after the marketing slide.

So what actually works?

  • Treat AI as an ecosystem play, not a logo exchange.
  • Co-create GTM, don't go alone when you don't have to.
  • Define ownership early and stick to it.
  • Accept that what's "best practice" today may be out-of-date by Monday.
