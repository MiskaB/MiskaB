The AI Partnership Puzzle: Assembling Without a Manual

Miska Braun November 27, 2025 6 min read AI

My today's two cents on AI.

I've spent the past year not learning to code better (that's still on the to-do list and I still can't really write code), but in back-to-back AI conversations with Microsoft, GitHub, AWS, and a whole ecosystem of partners. I've seen the excitement, the confusion, the chaos, and the magic. And I have to say: it's been a wild ride so far and constantly getting wilder.

And honestly? It often feels like we're all trying to assemble IKEA furniture… except nobody has the manual. Or they had it, lost it, or used it to level a table or other funky things really.

Everyone wants "One AI for me please." Very few are ready for the realities. Here goes:

Ownership chaos

AI sits in every department and in none of them really. This means that it is really hard to actually get anything done, especially in the larger organisations. Either it is done in pockets and silos or then it is total "free for all" meaning that there is no control, there is no tracability and there definetly is not optimization and governance.

  • IT: "We have the platform, so it's ours."
  • Developers: "Agents! We have them. They have us."
  • Security: "Put it in a concrete vault immediately and seal it."
  • Management: "AI, but safe, fast, magical… and preferably free."

I've even seen also these same teams arguing about who owns AI before discussing what they actually want to achieve with it. Then again there are bunch of companies offering advices on how to work with AI and what it "should" achieve for any given company.

GTM for product + services = spicy chaos for customers

Product companies want neat, packaged AI powered solutions that "just onboard instantly" and "scale" and provide the ROI clearly and are measurable. Services companies want to consult,explore, customise, and fix the mess underneath and help the customer to achieve that ROI, clarity and be experts in achieving business objectives.

AI sits in the middle whispering: "Good luck aligning those sales and GTM motions" when they are clearly competing with each other.

This slows more deals than any skill gap or tech gap ever has done. Well, atleast if we are no being clear about what we are attempting to accomplish, where our GTM do actually align and ultimately who are trying to serve and how to best align the previous. Instead of doing "discussions dance" for several meetings, it might be better to just be open about objectives and set the scene from the start.

AI moves faster than governance

Solution partners want to co-sell and sell solutions and services.

Tech vendors want to scale their products and reach new customers.

Customers want guarantees nobody can actually give, about the ROI, security, reliability, and basically everything else that matters after the marketing slide.

Customer internal teams align on a conference call about the need for AI, go to hackathon, get excited… and then realise they forgot to ask about ownwership, pricing, delivery, support, governance or basically everything that matters after the marketing slide or hackathon results and winners.

So what actually works (based on my limited experience)?

  • Treat AI as an ecosystem play, not a logo exchange or "cool new things".
  • Co-create GTM, don't go alone when you don't have to, create win-win-win cases.
  • Define ownership, agree limits and set responsibility early and stick to it.
  • Accept that what's "best practice" today may be out-of-date by Monday, be ready to make changes.
